Background

What is this all about?


This is the result of a project we did part of a data science course we took at Harvard during the Fall of 2015. Data science and machine learning play an increasing role in applied finance. Financial markets produce huge and ever increasing volumes of data, which calls for novel methods to discover and explore patterns, and possibly exploit them.


Beating the market, however, is challenging. Countless methods have been proposed to do so. In general, they are not more profitable than the "buy-and-hold" strategy, especially when transaction costs are considered. Nevertheless, we decided to give it a try!

Our approach

  • 1. Market sector selection

    Instead of trying to predict individual stocks, we focus on sectors of the U.S. market, such as real estate, technology, energy, industrials and others. We try to predict the evolution of index funds tracking each of these sectors.

  • 2. Technical analysis

    Using historical data from Yahoo! Finance, we compute various technical indicators related to our sector of interest, as well as indicators related to the broader stock market. We use these indicators (some quantitative, some binary) as features to feed our machine learning models.

  • 3. Sentiment analysis

    For selected sectors, we conduct natural language analysis of material published in the New York Times. For every trading day, we assess the general sentiment as good or bad, and use this as an additonal feature in our models.

  • 4. Classifier selection

    We train, optimize, validate, and test an ensemble of machine learning classifiers on our data. It includes logistic regression, support vector machines, random forest, extra trees, and naive Bayes classifiers. We then use the ensemble to predict if a market sector will be up (+) or down (-) in 14 days. We don't make predictions on the next trading day because we want to produce predictions that are less volatile and won't require making hundreds of trades a year.

  • 5. Trading simulation

    We calculate how much profits (or losses) we could make if we invested 10,000$ in an indexed fund on Jan 1st 2015 and kept it until December (buy-and-hold strategy). We compare this to how much we would make if we bought (or sold) our positions according to our classifier predictions.
